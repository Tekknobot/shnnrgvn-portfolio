About Us

Feels Like Old Times

Sports clippings, hanging plants, a sleepy cat, and the same steady pair of hands. Vito’s is the kind of room you settle into—not just pass through.

Inside these tiled floors and red vinyl chairs, the pace is slower. The TV murmurs from the corner, scissors move with a practiced rhythm, and regulars sink into their usual seats as if they’ve never left.

Vito is kind and unhurried—happy to talk hockey or let the room stay quiet while the cut takes shape. The shop cat drifts from sunbeam to sunbeam, occasionally stopping to inspect a new pair of shoes. It’s a Bloordale fixture that still feels like Saturday afternoon in another decade.

Find Your Way to the Chair

Look for the barber pole colours and gold lettering in the window. When the door’s propped open and the TV glow spills onto the sidewalk, you’re in the right place.

Vito’s sits on the south side of Bloor Street West, a short walk from Lansdowne Station. There’s bike parking out front and street parking along the neighbouring blocks.
