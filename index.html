Background

About Me

I was born in Jaffna, Sri Lanka and shortly later immigrated to Toronto, Canada, where I finished high school and studied Graphic Design at Seneca@York. My path into the creative and tech world hasn’t been straightforward — breaking into the industry and keeping momentum was difficult, and for a while I stepped away from it entirely.

That time away became a turning point. Over the years, I taught myself to code, slowly building up the skills I wished I had when I first started: thinking in components, designing for clarity, and shaping interfaces that feel intentional. What began as “learning to code” grew into a full shift toward front-end development, blending my design background with modern JavaScript and a focus on clean, well-crafted UI.

Now, I’m confident in where I am and focused on what comes next. I approach every project with curiosity, patience, and a belief that great digital work comes from caring about the details — the small choices that make an experience feel polished. The story isn’t finished yet, but this is the strongest chapter I’ve written so far.

Case Studies

WebDesk — Desktop-Style Home Screen

Role · Front-End Developer & UI Design

WebDesk started as a way to make the browser feel more like an operating system: a calm, organized “home base” for links and tools instead of a blank new tab.

I focused on clear hierarchy, a desktop-like grid, and small motion details that make opening panels and cards feel smooth but not distracting. The layout is fully responsive, so the interface feels structured on large monitors and still usable on smaller laptop screens.

Interface Design Responsive Layout Component Thinking
  • Goal: Create a calm, desktop-style starting point for daily browsing.
  • Approach: Card-based layout, subtle hover states, and consistent spacing system.
  • What I worked on: Layout, visual design, animations, and front-end implementation.
  • Result: A focused, app-like home screen that feels familiar and easy to scan.

Geopolitics Analysis Dashboard

Role · Front-End Developer & UX Layout

This dashboard explores how local events can be viewed in a global context for analysts. The challenge was to display a lot of information without overwhelming the user.

I split the interface into clear regions: filters and controls, event lists, and contextual detail views. Each section is designed to support fast scanning, with typographic hierarchy and spacing doing most of the visual work.

Dashboards Information Hierarchy Data UI
  • Goal: Help users move from raw events to clear, high-level understanding.
  • Approach: Strong hierarchy, sectioned layout, and clear visual separation of states.
  • What I worked on: Structure, layout, and interface polish for key views.
  • Result: A dashboard that feels dense but readable, with clear paths to insight.

NFL Calendar & Matchup Helper

Role · Front-End Developer & Interaction Design

This project focuses on quickly browsing NFL games by week and day, then drilling into specific matchups. The main constraint was making it feel fast and natural on mobile.

I designed a simple flow: pick a week, pick a day, see a clean agenda-style list of games. From there, tapping a matchup reveals details like kickoff time and status in a layout that still feels compact and thumb-friendly.

Mobile-First Interaction Design Sports UI
  • Goal: Make checking upcoming games feel quick and low-friction on mobile.
  • Approach: Step-by-step navigation (week → day → game) and clear typography.
  • What I worked on: Mobile layout, micro-interactions, and front-end build.
  • Result: A simple tool that feels more like an app than a static schedule page.
